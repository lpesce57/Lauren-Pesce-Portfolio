Maize & Blue Cupboard Website Redesign

Description: Year-long UX capstone turning a static pantry site into an accessible, task-focused experience.

Class: SI 487 (execution • research in SI 407)

Tools Utilized: Figma, Miro, Axe, WAVE, Google Forms, Zoom

Project Duration: Aug 2024 – Apr 2025

My Role: UX researcher & interaction designer on a 4-person team (led usability testing & IA redesign)

Problem Statement

The legacy site’s dense copy, confusing labels, and accessibility gaps kept students from quickly finding food-shopping hours, eligibility details, or volunteer information. A ground-up redesign was needed to support MBC’s expanding services and improve equity of access.

About the Client / Course

Maize & Blue Cupboard is the University of Michigan’s on-campus food pantry. Our capstone partnered directly with MBC staff to deliver a production-ready design that mirrors the pantry’s welcoming in-person experience.

Website Analysis

We conducted 49-response surveys, four student interviews, six first-time-user tests, and a full WCAG 2.2 accessibility audit. Patterns emerged: appointment links were hidden, FAQs were overwhelming, and 15 contrast & alt-text errors blocked screen-reader flow.

Analysis Methods

  • Likert-scale survey to prioritise features
  • Thematic coding of interview transcripts
  • Axe / WAVE scans for accessibility issues
  • Baseline vs. prototype task timing & error counts

Design

Insights flowed into low-fi sketches, mid-fi wireframes, and a hi-fi prototype. Key changes included a six-page IA, a persistent “Make an Appointment” CTA, and a “How to Help” mega-menu that separates Donate, Volunteer, and Host a Drive.

Design Evolution

Early homepages overloaded users with text. Iterations introduced a clean carousel, a smaller hero image, and a floating donate button. Dropdown glitches were rebuilt for full keyboard support.

Key Design Decisions

  • Appointment button visible on every page
  • Collapsible, searchable FAQ sections
  • Real-time volunteer slot panel & event pop-ups
  • High-contrast palette that meets WCAG AA

Final Result

A/B testing showed task success up 12 pp (88 % → 100 %) and average completion time cut from 23 s to 10 s; errors dropped 71 %. MBC stakeholders approved the design for hand-off to Student Life Web Services. Read the full final report (PDF).
